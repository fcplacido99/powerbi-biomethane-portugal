# Insights Summary

This document summarises the main analytical insights visible in the BioRede+ dashboard and supported by the provided 2024 and 2015–2024 average datasets.

## 1. National biomethane potential is sizeable and relatively stable

In the **technical-potential** view:

- **2024 total potential:** approximately **280,017 kNm³**
- **2015–2024 average:** approximately **277,909 kNm³**

The 2024 technical potential is about **0.8% higher** than the decade-average view, indicating that the broad national opportunity remains stable across the two dashboard perspectives.

The municipal average is approximately:
- **1,007 kNm³** in 2024;
- **1,000 kNm³** for the 2015–2024 average.

---

## 2. A limited set of municipalities concentrates the strongest production opportunities

The highest 2024 technical-potential municipalities are led by:

1. **Beja** — about **6,535 kNm³**
2. **Serpa** — about **5,824 kNm³**
3. **Coruche** — about **5,804 kNm³**

The 10-year average ranking changes slightly, with **Coruche**, **Beja** and **Santarém** leading.  
This suggests that some high-potential territories are structurally important across years, while others are more sensitive to annual feedstock variation.

---

## 3. Bovine residues and corn residues dominate technical potential

For the **2024 technical-potential** dashboard view, the dominant feedstock contributions are:

- **Bovine residues:** **37.2%**
- **Corn residues:** **29.1%**
- **Olive prunings:** **11.2%**

Together, bovine and corn residues represent roughly **66.3%** of technical production potential.

In the **2015–2024 average** view, bovine residues become even more prominent at about **43.2%**, reinforcing the importance of livestock-linked feedstocks in the national biomethane opportunity.

---

## 4. Cost competitiveness is spatially selective

The cost dashboard shows that the municipalities with the lowest normalised production cost are not necessarily the same municipalities with the highest production potential.

In the **2024, CHP + Best Practice** cost view, the most cost-efficient municipalities include:
- **Benavente**
- **Vila do Conde**
- **Barcelos**

This highlights the distinction between:
- **resource-rich municipalities**, and
- **deployment-efficient municipalities**.

For policy and investment prioritisation, both dimensions matter.

---

## 5. CAPEX is the dominant cost component

Under the **CHP + Best Practice** cost scenario:

- **2024:** CAPEX accounts for approximately **74.4%** of total cost.
- **2015–2024 average:** CAPEX accounts for approximately **75.8%** of total cost.

This suggests that scale, equipment requirements and plant configuration are central to competitiveness, while OPEX and logistics remain important but secondary in the aggregate cost breakdown.

---

## 6. Feasibility improves for only a subset of municipalities

Using the dashboard's feasibility condition:

```text
LCOE < Reference Gas Price
```

the uploaded data indicate that, under the **CHP + Best Practice** configuration:

- **22 of 278 municipalities** meet the condition in 2024;
- **31 of 278 municipalities** meet the condition in the 2015–2024 average view.

This reinforces the dashboard's role as a **screening and prioritisation tool**, rather than implying uniform competitiveness across mainland Portugal.

---

## 7. GHG performance improves substantially under Best Practice + CHP

In the **2024 CHP + Best Practice** GHG view:

- **Median carbon intensity:** approximately **-57.84 g CO₂-eq/MJ**
- **Baseline carbon intensity:** approximately **30.54 g CO₂-eq/MJ**
- **Difference:** approximately **-88.38 g CO₂-eq/MJ**

The same view shows:
- **Net emission impact:** approximately **-323.49 kt CO₂-eq/yr**
- **Baseline net impact:** approximately **273.36 kt CO₂-eq/yr**
- **Scenario improvement:** approximately **-596.85 kt CO₂-eq/yr**

The dashboard therefore shows a strong climate-performance improvement when advanced operational practice and CHP integration are selected.

---

## 8. The decade-average GHG signal is even stronger

In the **2015–2024 average, CHP + Best Practice** view:

- carbon intensity reaches approximately **-82.69 g CO₂-eq/MJ**;
- net emission impact reaches approximately **-596.86 kt CO₂-eq/yr**.

This suggests that the beneficial GHG signal is not confined to a single-year snapshot.

---

## 9. Overall interpretation

BioRede+ demonstrates that biomethane deployment in mainland Portugal is:

- **resource-relevant**, with substantial national production potential;
- **spatially uneven**, with clear municipal and regional differentiation;
- **cost-sensitive**, especially to capital intensity and plant/project configuration;
- **climate-positive under stronger operational scenarios**, particularly when evaluated against the Standard Practice + No CHP baseline.

The dashboard is therefore best understood as a **territorial prioritisation and scenario-analysis tool** for renewable gas planning.
